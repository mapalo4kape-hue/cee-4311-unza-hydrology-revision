## 1. Objective
Reverse-engineer the global software ecosystem serving agriculture, mining, construction, environmental, logistics, procurement, ERP, GIS, IoT and field operations across 8 regions; catalogue products, compare them, and identify **evidence-backed market gaps**. **No solution recommendations, no AgriMine Nexus design, no favouring; landscape only.**

## 2. Method
1. Web-researched the least-documented, highest-value segments (African/Zambian agri & fintech) and category leaders (mining, construction, EHS, GIS/drone/IoT, ERP, commodity/mobile money).
2. Registered **16 new sources** (`SRC-0071`–`SRC-0086`).
3. Built the **Global Competitor Catalogue** (153 products, `CMP-0001`–`CMP-0153`) and all 12 deliverables.
4. Extended the knowledge graph with findings `F-0062`–`F-0066` and edges `E-0109`–`E-0115`.

## 4. Key findings
- Landscape is **deep but domain-siloed and bifurcated**: global leaders feature-rich/high-cost/online-first/single-domain; African tools affordable/offline-USSD/mobile-first but shallow & fragmented.
- **No product** spans the productive industries on a shared backbone or unifies Zambia's digital rails.
- **Pricing bimodality** leaves the Zambian mid-market underserved; **interoperability** is the recurring pain/trend.
- 10 evidence-backed market gaps (GAP-X01..X10) and 10 innovation directions (IO-01..10) documented — as gaps, not solutions.
