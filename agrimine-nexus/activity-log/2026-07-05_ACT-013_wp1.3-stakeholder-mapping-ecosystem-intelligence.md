## 1. Objective
Build a comprehensive stakeholder ecosystem: identify every organisation/institution/actor across agriculture, mining, construction, environmental, and logistics in Zambia, with roles, authority, relationships, decision power, information, workflows, regulatory obligations, pain points, dependencies, and influence. Evidence-first; **no software design, database schemas, functional-requirement extraction, or business strategy.**

## 2. Method
Assembled the stakeholder ecosystem primarily from the **established, cited evidence base** (`SRC-0001`–`SRC-0107`, gathered across ACT-006→ACT-012 covering government, regulators, industry, financial, technology, and education actors), linking each stakeholder to documented workflows and regulations. Extended the knowledge graph with finding `F-0074`, organisation/authority nodes (`ORG-*`), and relationship/influence edges (`E-0144`–`E-0155`).

## 4. Key findings
- **75 stakeholders / 10 categories**; ~27 government/regulatory bodies form the compliance backbone; **ZEMA, ZRA, Data Protection Commissioner cross-cut all industries**; **mobile money + ZIAMIS are the widest digital rails**.
- **Every stakeholder maps to ≥1 documented workflow** and to regulations (completing the actor layer of the operational model).
- Influence concentrated in Critical actors (finance/rights/tax/monetary/production); smallholders/field actors hold high collective but low individual influence.
