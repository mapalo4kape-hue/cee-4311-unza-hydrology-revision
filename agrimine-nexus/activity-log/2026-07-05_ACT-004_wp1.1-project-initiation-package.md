## 1. Objective
Act as a research consultant and produce the WP1.1 **Project Initiation Package** — the plan that defines *how* National Intelligence research will be executed *before* any data collection begins.

## 2. Change of direction (why this activity exists)
The client redirected the project away from producing further large governance documents toward **consultant-style execution**: define the research package first, then execute, review, and move on. This activity implements that pivot. The previously committed `ACT-003` (Research Engine) is retained as-is (it is the machinery the PIP plugs into) and is **not** expanded; this PIP is logged as `ACT-004` to keep the append-only activity log intact.

## 3. Scope & exclusions
- **In scope:** authoring the PIP (10 sections) for WP1.1; updating the tracker and QA gate log; this record.
- **Excluded:** any data collection or findings — the PIP asserts no statistics. Named institutions are candidate sources to verify at collection.

## 4. Method — how the activity was carried out
1. Re-read the MREP WP1.1 definition (12 topics + deliverables) and the Research Engine standards the PIP must comply with (templates, metadata, evidence register, RTM, dashboard, QA gates).
2. Framed 10 objectives (O1–O10) mapping to the 12 WP1.1 topics.
3. Defined explicit in-scope / out-of-scope boundaries (deferring industry, technology, competitor, and business detail to later stages) plus assumptions/constraints.
4. Authored **~250 structured research questions** across 13 thematic groups (demographics, national economy, provincial/district economy, transport, energy, water, telecoms, financial inclusion, digital maturity, trade & logistics, climate/environment, policy, data landscape), each with a traceable ID prefix.
5. Specified 13 concrete deliverables (D1–D13) with repository locations, and the expected structured outputs (tables, maps, GIS layers, datasets, knowledge-graph entries, requirement/opportunity candidates).
6. Compiled a tiered **Required Sources** list of real Zambian institutions and multilateral/open/geospatial sources — explicitly flagged as candidate targets to verify, honouring evidence-first discipline.
7. Answered the four stakeholder questions (owns/uses/regulates/validates) at national level.
8. Built a risk table (missing/conflicting/outdated/biased data, access, scope creep, units) with mitigations, and a 10-point acceptance-criteria set tied to the 8 QA gates.
9. Wrote the phased activity plan (Phase 0–5) mapping future activities (ACT-005 planning, ACT-006+ collection).
10. Updated the Work Package Tracker (WP1.1 → *Initiation, Gate 1 review*) and opened `QA-0001` in the QA gate log; logged this activity; committed and pushed.
