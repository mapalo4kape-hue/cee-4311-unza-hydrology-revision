## 1. Objective
For every workflow from ACT-008, identify and structure the information ecosystem: what information is created, received, updated, stored, exchanged, reported; who owns/modifies/consumes it; retention; and how it is currently recorded. **No crops, software design, schemas, APIs, UML, features, or competitor analysis.**

## 2. Method
1. Reused the 41 ACT-008 workflows as the backbone; grounded record content in new authoritative sources (ZIAMIS farmer registry, Agricultural Credits Act warehouse-receipt contents, weather-index insurance).

3. Produced 6 catalogues (master data, transactions, documents, measurements, events, relationships), 3 matrices/registers (lifecycle, ownership, exchange), and a knowledge-graph expansion.
4. Expanded the graph with **16 entity instances**, findings `F-0053`–`F-0055`, and relationship edges `E-0072`–`E-0090` — every relationship evidence-based.

## 4. Key understanding established (cited)
- **Farmer master data** lives in the **ZIAMIS universal register** (bio, contact, NRC link, activities; unique digital ID; ~4.3M farmers) — geodata recommended but not standard.
- **Warehouse receipts** have a **legally-defined content set and retention** (Agricultural Credits Act No.35/2010; e-WRS + Collateral Registry under the 2026 Act).
- **Weather-index insurance** is managed via ZIAMIS for FISP crops.
- The information ecosystem is **digital on a few rails** (ZIAMIS, mobile money, SMS, WRS, ZAMACE) but **largely paper/verbal** for field, agronomic, harvest, and most transactional data — with **no standardised retention** outside legal/programme frameworks.
