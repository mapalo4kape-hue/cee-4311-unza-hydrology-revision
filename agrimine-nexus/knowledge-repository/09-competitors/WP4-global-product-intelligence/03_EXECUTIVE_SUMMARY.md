# WP4 — Global Product & Competitor Intelligence: Executive Summary

## Purpose
Build an evidence-first intelligence repository of software products relevant to AgriMine Nexus across Agriculture, Mining, Construction, Environmental, Logistics, and Enterprise — a **product reverse-engineering** exercise, not a marketing comparison. No AgriMine design, feature recommendations, requirement extraction, or subjective ranking.

## What was produced (builds on ACT-011)
- **Global Product Catalogue extended to 248 products** (`CMP-0001`–`CMP-0248`), ~19 attributes each. This pass added **95 net-new products** concentrated in previously thin categories: mining suites (Deswik, Vulcan, Datamine, Micromine, Hexagon, Surpac, RPMGlobal, Seequent, Pitram, Modular Mining, Wenco), CMMS/EAM (Maximo, Fiix, UpKeep, MaintainX), EHS/ESG/carbon (Cority, Intelex, Enablon, Sphera, Persefoni, Watershed), LIMS/environmental data (LabWare, STARLIMS, SENAITE, Locus, EarthSoft, Aquarius), construction (Procore, Autodesk Construction Cloud, Bentley, Tekla, Primavera, Aconex, CostX, RIB Candy/BuildSmart), logistics (Manhattan, Blue Yonder, SAP TM/EWM, Oracle OTM/WMS, Descartes, project44, FourKites, Samsara, Geotab, Cartrack, MiX Telematics), and enterprise/HR/low-code (Sage, Palladium, Workday, ServiceNow, Appian, OutSystems, Mendix, Power Platform).
- **Southern African & Zambian coverage strengthened** — RIB Candy/BuildSmart, Sage Pastel/Palladium/Omni, SYSPRO, Cartrack, MiX Telematics; Zambian Probase, Lupiya, PremierCredit, Union54/Broadpay, eShandi, ZIAMIS, Smart Zambia, Infratel, MNO USSD rails.
- **Coverage matrices & registers** (Outputs 3–11): feature, workflow, stakeholder, technology-adoption, integration; pricing, deployment, strength–weakness, and market-gap registers — all mapped to prior `WF-/STK-/REG-/TECH-` IDs.
- **Zambia & Southern Africa market analysis** (Outputs 12–13) — vendors, government systems, adoption barriers, pricing/infrastructure constraints, regional structure.
- KG updates (`F-0079`–`F-0084`, category nodes, edges `E-0168`–`E-0179`); evidence `SRC-0114`–`SRC-0117` (total `SRC-0001`–`SRC-0117`).

## Headline conclusions (evidence-based)
1. **Southern Africa is the regional software hub** (RIB, Sage, SYSPRO, Cartrack, MiX) serving SADC including Zambia; global Tier-1 vendors reach the region mainly through large-enterprise/mine deployments and partners.
2. **Agri-tech innovation is East/West-African-led** (Kenya/Nigeria) with active Southern-African expansion into Zambia (Apollo, Aerobotics, Hello Tractor).
3. **The Zambian accessible tier is offline-first, mobile/USSD, and low-cost/OSS**; Tier-1 commercial suites are constrained by cost, connectivity, and skills.
4. **Mining/BIM suites are deep but desktop-bound, costly, and multi-vendor** — weak on mobile/offline-field use relevant to Zambian conditions.

## Discipline honoured
Documented evidence only. No AgriMine feature design, requirement extraction, architecture, or subjective competitor ranking. Capabilities not asserted without a catalogue evidence source; unverified items flagged `K`.

## Honest limitations (see market gap register)
- **Quantity floors partially met:** 248 products vs. ≥300 target; ~150 commercial (≥100 met); ~15 OSS vs. ≥50; ~50 African (met); ~20 Zambian (met) (`GAP-M02`).
- Many African/Zambian entries are **known-but-unverified** (`GAP-M01`); government/NGO/university systems under-evidenced (`GAP-M03/M04/M05`).
- Pricing captured at model level, not exact prices (`GAP-M06`); per-product feedback/awards/certifications and unsolved-pain-point mapping are category-level, not exhaustive per product (`GAP-M07/M08`).
