# Executive Summary — Global Product & Competitor Intelligence (D12)

## Scope covered
Catalogued **153 software products** (`CMP-0001`–`CMP-0153`) across all 28 requested categories and 8 regions, including **50+ African products** and **25+ Zambia-relevant** products (agri, fintech, commodity, e-gov), grounded in this session's web research plus review/analyst evidence.

## The landscape in one paragraph
The global software ecosystem serving agriculture, mining, construction, environmental, logistics, procurement, ERP, GIS, IoT and field operations is **deep but domain-siloed and geographically bifurcated**. Global leaders (Procore, Autodesk, Esri, SAP, IBM Maximo, Maptek/Deswik/Hexagon, Cority/Enablon) are **feature-rich, high-cost, online-first, and single-domain**. African/Zambian tools (AgriPredict, eMsika, Lima Links, Apollo, Twiga, Hello Tractor, ZAMACE, mobile money) are **affordable, offline/USSD-capable, and mobile-first, but operationally shallow and fragmented**. No product spans the productive industries on a shared operational backbone, and none unifies Zambia's existing digital rails (mobile money, ZIAMIS, Mining Cadastre, ZPPA e-GP, My-WARMA/ZEMA, ZAMACE/Collateral Registry).

## Key evidence-backed findings
1. **Domain silos** — mining planning vs fleet management; construction PM vs ERP; agriculture advisory vs operations; environmental compliance stands apart (F-0063, F-0064).
2. **Pricing bimodality** — ~$5 smallholder apps vs ~$30k–$100k+ enterprise suites, with little serving the **Zambian mid-market** (D3).
3. **Offline/USSD depth gap** — African tools are offline-capable but shallow; enterprise tools are deep but online-only (D4).
4. **Integration is the recurring theme** — vendors themselves cite interoperability as the trend and pain; open APIs are common but no local integration fabric exists (D5, F-0065).
5. **Recurring customer pain points** — cost, long implementations, complexity, weak offline, poor localisation, fragmentation (D7).

## Evidence-backed market gaps (not solutions)
Cross-industry unified platform (GAP-X01); affordable mid-market tier (GAP-X04); offline-first operational depth (GAP-X02); **local integration fabric** (GAP-X03); regulatory-compliance automation (GAP-X05); commodity finance + field ops (GAP-X06); shared master data (GAP-X08); localised low-literacy UX (GAP-X09); field-to-finance traceability (GAP-X10). Full detail in D8–D10.
