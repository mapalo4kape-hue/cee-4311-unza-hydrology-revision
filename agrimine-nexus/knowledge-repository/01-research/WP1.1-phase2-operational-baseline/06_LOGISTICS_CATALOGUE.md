# Logistics Catalogue — Zambia

> Zambia is landlocked; corridors connect it to regional sea ports. Source: [SRC-0041] (UN OHRLLS/GRZ, pub. 2024; confidence 84/100; retrieved 2026-07-05) unless noted.

## 1. Regional transport corridors
- **North-South Corridor** (to Durban) · **Dar es Salaam Corridor** (Tanzania) · **Beira Corridor** (Mozambique) · **Nacala Corridor** (Mozambique/Malawi) · **Walvis Bay–Ndola–Lubumbashi Corridor** (Namibia) · **Lobito Corridor** (Angola, emerging) · **Central Corridor**.
- TAZARA railway (to Dar es Salaam) under revitalisation; Lobito rail provides an Atlantic route for Copperbelt/North-Western minerals.

## 2. Border posts / One-Stop Border Posts (OSBPs)
- **Chirundu** (Zimbabwe; first OSBP, model law adopted regionally), **Nakonde** (Tanzania), **Kasumbalesa** (DRC; noted as a bottleneck), **Kazungula** (Botswana; bridge 2021), **Mwami/Mchinji** (Malawi), **Katima-Mulilo** (Namibia).

## 3. Cross-border trade & procedures
- Zambia has signed/ratified corridor agreements (Beira, Central, North-South, Nacala) and Nacala road/rail transport agreements with Mozambique & Malawi.
- Government "Land-Linked Zambia" strategy develops logistics hubs, dry ports, agro-processing zones, and industrial parks along corridors.

## 4. Recorded uncertainties (→ Gap Register)
- Freight/cargo-volume statistics, border dwell times, logistics-performance index scores (GAP-117).
- Inventory of major logistics companies, cold-chain and warehousing capacity, dry ports (GAP-118).
- Import/export procedure detail and customs digitisation status (ZRA) (GAP-119).
