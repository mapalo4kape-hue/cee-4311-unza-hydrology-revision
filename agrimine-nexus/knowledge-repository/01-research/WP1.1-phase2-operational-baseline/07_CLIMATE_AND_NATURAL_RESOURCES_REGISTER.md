# Climate and Natural Resources Register — Zambia

## 1. Agro-ecological zones (AER)
Source: [SRC-0042] (FAO, pub. 2020; confidence 85/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).

| Zone | Rainfall (annual) | Growing season | Land share | Notes |
|------|-------------------|----------------|-----------|-------|
| **AER I** (south; Zambezi/Luangwa valleys, parts of Southern/Western) | **< 800 mm** | 80–120 days | ~12% | Driest; most drought-prone; drought-resistant crops |
| **AER II** (centre & east; IIa fertile plateau, IIb Kalahari sands) | **800–1,000 mm** | 100–140 days | — | Main commercial farming belt |
| **AER III** (north & west) | **1,000–1,500 mm** | 130–160 days | ~46% | Wettest; largest land area; maize & high-yield crops |

- Seasons: wet **Nov–Apr**; dry **May–Oct** (cool-dry May–Aug, hot-dry Sep–Oct) [SRC-0043] (MDPI/FAO, pub. 2025; confidence 82/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).

## 2. Rainfall & temperature (recent)
- 2023/2024 rainy season among the driest on record: national rainfall **35–45% below the 1991–2020 mean** (Zone I deficits >50%); mean temperatures 1.5–2.0 °C above baseline (ZMD) [SRC-0043].

## 3. Drought & flood history
- Notable droughts: 1991–92, 2015–16, 2019–20, and the severe **2023/2024** event [SRC-0043].
- **National emergency declared 29 Feb 2024**; drought affected **84 of 116 districts** [SRC-0043].
- Lake Kariba storage fell to ~**2.89% of usable capacity** by Dec 2024, cutting hydropower [SRC-0043].

## 4. Hydrological basins
- Six major river basins: **Zambezi, Kafue, Luangwa, Chambeshi, Luapula, Tanganyika** — all within the Zambezi and Congo transboundary basins [SRC-0044] (FAO, pub. 2024; confidence 85/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).
- Zambia holds >40% of Southern African Power Pool water resources; ~6,000 MW unexploited hydropower potential (energy register, SRC-0034).

## 5. Meteorological / hydrological infrastructure
- Zambia Meteorological Department (ZMD) operates the national station network (station sparsity noted in Phase 1); satellite/reanalysis (CHIRPS/ERA5/NASA POWER) supplement (Master Source Register).

## 6. Recorded uncertainties (→ Gap Register)
- Station-level rainfall/temperature normals per province; agro-ecological zone GIS boundaries (GAP-120).
- Flood-risk zonation and basin water-balance data (WARMA) (GAP-121).
- Soil and land-cover datasets (GAP-122).
