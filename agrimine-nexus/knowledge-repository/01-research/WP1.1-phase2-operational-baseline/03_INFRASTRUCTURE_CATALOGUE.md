# Infrastructure Catalogue — Zambia

## 1. Roads
- Total classified road network **67,671 km**; **Core Road Network 40,454 km** (Road Development Agency) [SRC-0035] (RDA, pub. 2024; confidence 80/100; retrieved 2026-07-05) — RDA primary document to confirm.

## 2. Rail, air, borders
- Rail: Zambia Railways (domestic) and **TAZARA** (to Dar es Salaam) undergoing revitalisation; **Lobito Corridor** rail to Atlantic via Angola/DRC being developed [SRC-0041] (UN OHRLLS/GRZ, pub. 2024; confidence 84/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).
- Border posts / One-Stop Border Posts: **Chirundu, Nakonde, Kasumbalesa, Kazungula, Mwami**; Kazungula Bridge (to Botswana) commissioned 2021 [SRC-0041].
- (Airport inventory — e.g., Kenneth Kaunda International — to be catalogued from ZCAA; Gap Register.)

## 3. Energy — generation
- Installed generation capacity **3,871.32 MW (2024)**, up from **3,811.32 MW (2023)** [SRC-0032] (Ministry of Energy, pub. 2024; confidence 90/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).
- Mix: **hydro ~82%**, coal ~9%, solar ~5%, HFO/diesel remainder [SRC-0032]; hydro-dominant, renewable >83% [SRC-0033].
- Peak demand ~**2,500 MW**; during the 2024 drought actual generation fell to ~**1,019 MW (Aug 2024)** / ~1,225 MW [SRC-0033] (World Bank/GRZ, pub. 2026; confidence 85/100; retrieved 2026-07-05); [SRC-0034] (ZIPAR, pub. 2025; confidence 85/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).
- Transmission lines **12,705 km (2023)** [SRC-0033]; target +6,200 MW capacity by 2030 [SRC-0033].
- Market: single-buyer model, ZESCO principal off-taker; IPPs via PPAs [SRC-0032].

## 4. Energy — access
- National electricity access **53.9% (2023 National Energy Access survey)**: urban **80%**, rural **34%** [SRC-0034]. Rural grid connection only ~5.6% of households [SRC-0033].
- **Contradiction recorded:** older World Bank data reported ~44.5% national access (2020) — different year/method (Gap Register).
- Rural Electrification Act **No. 5 of 2023** governs rural electrification [SRC-0034].

## 5. Water supply
- Access statistics and utilities to be catalogued from NWASCO/JMP primaries (Gap Register) — noted as pending; drought severely reduced water availability (see Climate register).

## 6. Telecommunications / internet
- Telecommunication **towers 3,626** and **sites 14,519 (2024)** [SRC-0036] (ZICTA, pub. 2025; confidence 80/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).
- 5G launched (MTN 2022, Airtel 2023); directive to phase out 2G/3G to make 4G the national standard [SRC-0038] (Freedom House, pub. 2024; confidence 78/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).
- (Detailed connectivity metrics in the Digital Ecosystem Register.)
