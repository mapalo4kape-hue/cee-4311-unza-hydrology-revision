# National Economic Evidence Register — Zambia

> Citation format: `[SRC-NN] (Org, pub. date; confidence NN/100; retrieved 2026-07-05)`. Figures are recorded as published; unit/base-year noted. No interpretation.

## 1. GDP & growth
- Real GDP grew **4.0% in 2024** and **5.4% in 2023** (constant 2010 prices) [SRC-0023] (ZamStats, pub. 2025; confidence 92/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).
- GDP at current prices: **K688,851 million (2024)** vs **K557,406 million (2023)** [SRC-0023].
- World Bank (factor cost) records growth ~5.7% (2023) and ~2.0% (2024e), with rebound projected 6.1% (2025f) [SRC-0024] (World Bank, pub. 2024; confidence 88/100; retrieved 2026-07-05). *(Method difference vs ZamStats noted.)*

## 2. Sector contribution to GDP (shares)
- 2023: **Agriculture ~2.2%**, **Industry ~35.1%**, **Services ~56.8%** [SRC-0025] (Statista/World Bank data, pub. 2025; confidence 75/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).
- Mining & quarrying ~**14.3% (2023)** [SRC-0025]; the top current-price contributors in 2024 were wholesale & retail trade, mining & quarrying, transport & storage, construction, manufacturing (collectively ~67.5%) [SRC-0023].
- Agriculture, forestry & fishing **contracted ~9.2% in 2024** (drought); electricity supply fell ~28.9% [SRC-0023].

## 3. Inflation & monetary
- Annual inflation **11.2% (Dec 2025)** [SRC-0028] (ZamStats, pub. 2025-12; confidence 92/100; retrieved 2026-07-05); **7.1% (Mar 2026)**, easing to 6.8% (April 2026), target band 6–8% [SRC-0029] (Bank of Zambia, pub. 2026; confidence 93/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).
- Average annual inflation was 11.1% in 2022 [SRC-0027]; World Bank records 10.9% (2023) and 15.0% (2024) [SRC-0024].

## 4. Employment & labour
- Labour-force participation **31.4%**; unemployment **13.1%** (female 14.9%, male 11.5%) — 2022 LCMS [SRC-0027] (ZamStats, pub. 2024; confidence 90/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).
- Agriculture, forestry & fishing employs the largest share of workers, **37.2%** [SRC-0027].
- World Bank labour data: participation ~35%, employment-to-population ~31%, with unemployment rising in 2024 among youth/women/rural [SRC-0030] (World Bank, pub. 2025; confidence 80/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).

## 5. Poverty & household income
- **60% of the population below the national poverty line** (rural 78.8%, urban 31.9%); **48% extremely poor** — 2022 LCMS [SRC-0027].
- International poverty rate ($2.15, 2017 PPP) ~63.7% (2023) [SRC-0024].
- Average monthly household income: rural **K2,112**, urban **K5,547** (2022 LCMS) [SRC-0027].

## 6. Trade & commodities
- **Copper is the dominant export**; economy vulnerable to global copper prices [SRC-0031] (Lloyds/World Bank/FAO, pub. 2025; confidence 72/100; retrieved 2026-07-05).
- FDI net inflow ~3.9% of GDP (2024e); current-account and fiscal balances recorded by World Bank [SRC-0024].

## 7. Agriculture output (macro)
- Only ~15% of arable potential is cultivated; 2024 drought cut cereal production to ~**1.9 million tonnes (−43% vs 5-yr average)**; maize ~1.6 Mt (~−50%) [SRC-0031].

## 8. Recorded uncertainties (→ Gap Register)
- Provincial/district GDP not published at fine resolution (GAP-101).
- MSME count, registered-business count, formal/informal firm split (GAP-102).
- Detailed exchange-rate history series and export/import commodity tables (retrieve BoZ/ZamStats/ZRA primaries) (GAP-103).
- 2023/2024 Labour Force Survey primary figures (participation/unemployment/informality) (GAP-104).
