# Stakeholder Encyclopedia, Directory, Gap Register & Executive Summary

## Output 2 — Stakeholder Encyclopedia (highlights)
> Narrative profiles are in the register (mandate/role, services, info, systems, obligations, workflows, pain points, influence). Key anchor profiles:
- **Ministry of Agriculture / ZIAMIS (STK-001):** owns FISP, extension, crop surveys; runs the national farmer registry (~4.3M farmers); critical to agriculture workflows.
- **Minerals Regulation Commission (STK-009):** new autonomous mining regulator (MRCA 2024) consolidating former directorates; critical to mining rights/safety/environment.
- **ZEMA (STK-010):** cross-cutting environmental authority (EIA/audits) touching all four industries.
- **ZRA (STK-020):** tax + customs (ASYCUDA); critical to logistics/export/finance.
- **Bank of Zambia + mobile money (STK-024/062):** payment rails underpinning agriculture, commodity, and cross-industry finance.
- **FRA + ZAMACE + warehouse operators (STK-026/027/038):** grain marketing & warehouse-receipt finance.

## Output 8 — Organisational Directory
The 75 stakeholders (`STK-001`–`STK-075`) in the register constitute the directory, grouped by category (Government, Regulators, Agriculture, Mining, Construction, Environmental, Logistics, Financial, Technology, Education/Research/Community), each with geography, mandate, systems, and evidence. Named individual organisations within some categories (all banks, all mines, all cooperatives) are a recorded gap.

## Output 19 — Executive Summary
- Mapped **75 stakeholders** across 10 categories spanning agriculture, mining, construction, environmental, logistics, plus government, regulators, financial, technology, and education/research ecosystems.
- **Every stakeholder participates in ≥1 documented workflow** (Output 10) and links to regulations (Output 11), completing the actor layer of the operational model.
- **~27 government/regulatory bodies** form the compliance backbone; **ZEMA, ZRA, and the Data Protection Commissioner cross-cut all industries**; **mobile money and ZIAMIS are the widest digital rails**.
- **Influence is concentrated** in a small set of Critical actors (finance/rights/tax/monetary/production) while **smallholders and field actors hold high collective but low individual influence** — a defining feature of the ecosystem.
- Collaboration surfaces already exist (FISP↔agro-dealers↔mobile money; FRA↔ZAMACE↔banks; ZEMA↔MRC; ZRA↔C&F↔corridors) — recorded as observations, **not strategy**.
