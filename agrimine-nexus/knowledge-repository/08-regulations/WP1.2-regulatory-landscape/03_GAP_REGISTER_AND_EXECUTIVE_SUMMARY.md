# Regulatory Landscape — Executive Summary & Gap Register (WP1.2)

## Output 17 — Executive Summary
- Catalogued **~37 primary legal instruments** (`REG-0001`–`REG-0037`) and **~21 regulatory authorities** governing the operational workflows across agriculture, mining, construction, environmental engineering, logistics, and cross-cutting domains.
- **Every major documented workflow maps to at least one regulation** (Output 11): mining rights→MRCA 2024; EIA→EMA 2011/SI 3-2026; contractor registration→NCC Act; warehouse receipts→Agricultural Credits Act; seed procurement→Plant Variety & Seeds Act; export→Customs & Excise; public procurement→PPA 2020; finance→FIC/AML; all data→Data Protection Act.
- **Cross-cutting compliance** (data protection, AML, tax, labour, competition, company/IP, standards) applies to **all** industries — reinforcing the unified operational model.
- **Recent reform wave (2024–2026):** MRCA 2024, GMDA 2025, Cyber Security/Crimes Acts 2025, OHS Act 2025, EIA Regulations SI 3 of 2026, Agricultural Credits & Warehouse Receipts Act 2026 — several still bedding in.
- **Compliance pattern:** register/licence → operate in scope → report periodically → submit to inspection/audit → renew; cross-border adds customs & preferential-origin rules.

## Honest note on quality target

This activity added **14 net-new primary legal sources** (`SRC-0094`–`SRC-0107`) at citation quality and **reused ~15 prior legal sources** (Constitution, DPA, MRCA, NCC, EMA/EIA, WRMA, Road Traffic, National Registration, EIZ, Ag Credits, Standards, Statistics, ASYCUDA).
